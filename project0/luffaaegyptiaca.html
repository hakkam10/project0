Family Cucurbitaceae
Genus Luffa
Species L. aegyptiaca
Habit

Usually climbing herbs, annual, mostly cultivated for vegetables.

Root

Tap, branched.

Stem

Prostrate, weak, herbaceous, climbing with 2-5 lid tendrils, pubescent or nearly glabrous, sap present. Usually angular..

Leaf

Large, cordate, petiolate (long petiole) multicostate reticulate, 5-7 lobed, nearly glabrous to slightly scabrid.

Inflorescence

Male flowers are arranged in racemes or in clusters on the top peduncles.

Female flowers are solitary. Monoecious.

Male Flower (Staminate flower)

Pedicellate, actinomorphic, regular, unisexual, incomplete.

Calyx: Five sepals, gamosepalous, campanulate, lobes spreading superior.

Corolla: Petals five, free but united below, spreading, obovate or obcordate, yellow or white, superior.

Androecium: Stamens three rarely five, filaments three or 5, free or connate, anthers extrorse, one 1-celled and the remaining, two 2-celled, anther cells sigmoid often the margin of the broad connective. Stamens inserted on the mouth of the calyx.

Gynoecium: Absent.

Female Flower (Pistillate flower)

Pedicellate, actinomorphic, regular, unisexual, epigynous, incomplete.

Calyx: Calyx tube produced shortly above the ovary.

Corolla: As in male flower.

Androecium: Absent. Sometimes staminodes present.

Gynoecium: Three carpels (tricarpellary), syncarpous; ovary inferior, elongated, angled or grooved, unilocular; placentation parietal, style cylindric; ovules many; stigma three-lobed.

Fruit

Berry, elongated, the rind becomes dry and paper like.

Seeds

Usually black, flattened, winged or wingless.

Generic Characters

Fruit a berry, oblong or cylindrical, the rind becomes dry and papery, dehiscent; corolla rotate or campanulate, 5-lobed to the base; male flowers arranged in racemes.
