Cucurbita sp.

Scientific Classification
Kingdom Plantae
Clade Tracheophytes
Clade Angiosperms
Clade Eudicots
Clade Rosids
Order Cucurbitales
Family Cucurbitaceae
Genus Cucurbita
Habit

Large climbing herbs, annual or perennial, hispid or hairy.

Leaf

Leaves petiolate, cordate, ovate, 5-angular or lobed 2 to 4 fid tendrils.

Flower

Flowers very large, yellow, solitary, monoecious.

Male Flower

Staminate flowers (male) generally long pedicellate; calyx tube campanulate, 5-lobed, linear or foliaceous; corolla gamopetalous, campanulate, 5-lobed hardly half way down; stamens apparently 3 in number, inserted on the calyx tube, filaments free, anthers connate, linear and united, one l-celled, two 2-celled cells conduplicate, ovary rudimentary.

Female Flower

Pistillate flowers (female) are short peduncled; calyx and corolla as in male; ovary oblong; 1-celled with 3 to 5 or many ovuled placentae; style short and thick with 3 to 5 stigmas; large epigynous cushion shaped 5-lobed disc.

Fruit

a pair of elongated follicles.
